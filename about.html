I'm an environmental data scientist and sustainability specialist with a background in spatial analysis, ESG systems design, and climate risk assessment. I hold a B.S. from Emory University (Environmental Sciences, GPA 3.72) and scored 329 on the GRE.

My work sits at the intersection of ecology and data engineering — whether that's building habitat suitability models from satellite imagery, automating ESG disclosure workflows, or translating physical climate hazards into capital planning inputs for global operations teams.

I've worked with barrier island ecosystems in Georgia, led sustainability assessments that placed organizations in the top 5% globally, and published peer-reviewed research on coastal change. I believe rigorous data practice is one of the most direct paths to better environmental outcomes.

Outside of work, I track shorebirds and loggerhead nests on Jekyll Island, explore music, and read widely across ecology, systems thinking, and design.
