Places That Shaped Me
Born School Lived Traveled

I’m Cassiel Chen — part sustainability strategist, part data storyteller, and a lifelong learner. I work at the intersection of corporate sustainability, climate regulatory compliance, and operations, where I turn complex sustainability data into clear, visual, and actionable insights. I enjoy bringing structure to ambiguity, whether that's building my company's first system for navigating climate disclosure regulations or establishing the framework of sustainability empowerment across the organization. At the core, I am driven by turning complexity into clarity and contextualizing sustainability for different groups of people.

My research is rooted in environmental data science and geospatial analysis, with a focus on translating physical and environmental systems into quantitative insights. At Emory University, I worked extensively with spatial data, remote sending, and statistical modeling. My research has included geobiochemical work examining soil processes and element cycling, as well as mapping shoreline change and modeling sea turtle nesting habitat suitability - experiences that shaped my ability to connect environmental processes with data-driven decision making.

Outside of work, I enjoy working out, cafe-hopping, and learning about all the random things in this world. I have a soft spot for vintage jewelry collectibles - I am drawn to pieces that carry the marks of time, and I enjoy restoring them, bringing them back into the light. This space is where I bring all of that together: work, curiosity, and the occasional rabbit hole.
