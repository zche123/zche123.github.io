Projects / Physical Climate Risk Framework

Climate Risk & ESG

Physical Climate Risk
Framework — Global
Manufacturer

TCFD-aligned assessment of physical climate risk across 200+ manufacturing and logistics facilities worldwide, mapping chronic and acute hazards under RCP 4.5 and 8.5 scenarios to prioritize adaptation investment and disclosure.

Context

As physical climate risks move from theoretical to financially material, large manufacturers face regulatory and investor pressure to quantify exposure across their asset base. This project was initiated in response to TCFD disclosure obligations and the need to integrate climate risk into long-term capital planning.

The scope covered 200+ owned and leased facilities across six continents, ranging from large assembly plants to regional distribution centers.

Hazard Screening

Seven physical hazards were screened for each facility under both a moderate (RCP 4.5) and high-emission (RCP 8.5) scenario at two time horizons — 2030 and 2050:

  • Chronic heat (increase in days above 35°C wet-bulb)
  • Precipitation change and pluvial flooding
  • Riverine and coastal flood inundation depth/frequency
  • Water stress and scarcity (Aqueduct WRI)
  • Wildfire risk index
  • Tropical cyclone wind and surge exposure
  • Sea-level rise (facility-level tidal gauge extrapolation)

Methodology

Facility coordinates were geocoded and matched to a combination of proprietary climate datasets (Four Twenty Seven / Moody's ESG) and open-source products (CHELSA climate projections, FATHOM flood maps, WRI Aqueduct, Global Windfield). A Python pipeline automated ingestion, spatial join, and scoring for all 200+ locations.

Risk scores were calculated as a function of hazard intensity, exposure (asset value, headcount), and sensitivity (facility type, redundancy). A heat-map matrix ranked facilities by composite risk tier (High / Medium / Low) at each scenario–horizon combination.

Outputs & Impact

The project produced a facility-level risk register integrated into the company's enterprise risk management system, scenario-based financial exposure estimates (revenue at risk, CapEx sensitivity) for TCFD disclosure, and a prioritized adaptation roadmap identifying the 12 highest-risk facilities for site-level resilience assessment.

Findings were presented to the CFO and Chief Sustainability Officer and incorporated into the company's annual sustainability report and CDP climate disclosure.
